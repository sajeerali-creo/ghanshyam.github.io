CTC LEAF

he method of processing black tea, in which leaves are passed through many cylindrical rollers, with sharp teeth that crush, tear & curl the tea, into tiny pellets, is called crush, tear & curl. Sometimes, this process is also referred to as cut, tear & curl. This method differs from the standard method in which tea leaves are rolled into strips. Tea thus produced is called CTC tea or Mamri chai. When brewed, CTC teas often produce a rich red- brown color. It's very popular in India. Today most black teas are produced by CTC method. CTC tea offers many health benefits. Assam CTC has many oxidants, which help to fight cancer, without damaging surrounding cells This tea keeps one hydrated. It can prevent heart problems, helps to cut down on fat and aids digestion.

How to prepare CTC tea

  • Add a cup of water to the pot & boil. Now, add 2 teaspoons of tea leaves & stir occasionally.
  • You may add a spice mix of ginger, cardamom, anise, fennel, cinnamon & star anise.
  • Continue to stir so that, the spices mix well. At the same time, bring the brew to a boil.
  • Simmer on low flame, for 5 minutes, to enhance the taste. Add sugar or honey.
  • Strain the tea & enjoy your invigorating cup.
